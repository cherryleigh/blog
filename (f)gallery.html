My Favorite Anime



Haikyuu

Haikyuu

Haikyuu!! (often written as Haikyū!!) is a popular Japanese sports anime and manga series created by Haruichi Furudate. It belongs to the comedy, coming-of-age, and sports genres and was first published as a manga in 2012, later becoming an anime TV series that began airing in 2014.
The story mainly follows Shoyo Hinata, a boy who dreams of becoming a great volleyball player even though he is shorter than most athletes. He joins the volleyball team at Karasuno High School and ends up playing alongside his former rival, Tobio Kageyama. The series focuses on their teamwork, rivalry, and growth as they aim to improve and compete against strong teams.
At its heart, Haikyuu!! is about determination, friendship, and teamwork. Hinata was inspired by a small professional volleyball player and works hard to overcome his limitations, showing that effort and passion can help someone chase big dreams.

Blue Spring Ride

Blue Spring Ride

Blue Spring Ride (Ao Haru Ride) is a Japanese coming-of-age romance story that follows the emotional journey of Futaba Yoshioka, a high school student trying to reshape her life after painful social experiences in middle school. When she was younger, other girls became jealous of the attention she received from boys, causing her to feel rejected and isolated. Determined not to repeat that experience, Futaba deliberately changes her behavior in high school, acting less traditionally feminine so she can fit in and avoid being excluded. Her carefully constructed identity begins to unravel when she reunites with Kou Tanaka—her first love—who had suddenly moved away years earlier without explanation. The Kou she meets again is no longer the gentle boy she remembers. Now going by the name Kou Mabuchi, he appears distant, cynical, and emotionally guarded after experiencing serious family hardships, including his mother’s illness and death. His personality shift leaves Futaba confused and determined to understand what happened during the years they were apart. As they slowly reconnect, the story explores Futaba’s efforts to rediscover her authentic self while building real friendships and facing complicated feelings that never fully disappeared. Their relationship becomes a central thread, showing how people can change while still carrying pieces of their past.

Jujutsu Kaisen

Jujutsu Kaisen

Jujutsu Kaisen (often called JJK) is a Japanese manga and anime series created by Gege Akutami. It blends adventure, dark fantasy, and supernatural elements and was originally serialized from 2018 to 2024, becoming one of the best-selling manga series with millions of copies worldwide. The story follows Yuji Itadori, a high school student whose life changes when he becomes involved in the hidden world of sorcery. After swallowing a cursed object—a finger belonging to the powerful curse Ryomen Sukuna—Yuji becomes Sukuna’s host and is drawn into a secret organization of Jujutsu Sorcerers who fight dangerous supernatural curses born from human negative emotions.

My Hero Academia

My Hero Academia

My Hero Academia (often shortened to MHA) is a Japanese manga and anime series written and illustrated by Kōhei Horikoshi. It falls under the adventure, science-fantasy, and superhero genres and was originally published from 2014 to 2024, later becoming a highly successful global franchise. The story is set in a world where most people are born with special superpowers called “Quirks.” Because these abilities are so common, heroism has become an official profession, with licensed heroes trained, regulated, and held responsible for how they use their powers. Young people who want to become heroes attend specialized schools, the most famous being U.A. High School, where students learn combat skills, rescue techniques, and teamwork.
